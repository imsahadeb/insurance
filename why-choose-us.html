Term Insurance

What is term insurance?

Term insurance is a type of life insurance that provides coverage for a specified period or "term," such as 10, 20, or 30 years. If the policyholder dies during the term, a death benefit is paid to the beneficiaries.

Who should consider term insurance?

Term insurance is ideal for individuals seeking affordable, temporary coverage to protect against financial obligations like a mortgage, education expenses, or income replacement for dependents.

What happens when the term ends?

When the term ends, the coverage expires. Policyholders may have the option to renew the policy, convert it to permanent insurance, or let it lapse. Renewing may result in higher premiums.

Can I convert my term insurance to a permanent policy?

Many term insurance policies offer the option to convert to a permanent policy without a medical exam. Check your policy's terms and conditions for specific details.

How are premiums determined for term insurance?

Premiums are based on factors such as the policyholder's age, health, lifestyle, and the length of the term. Term insurance premiums are generally lower than those for permanent life insurance.

Are term insurance premiums fixed?

Most term insurance policies have fixed premiums for the duration of the term. However, if you renew the policy at the end of the term, the new premiums will likely be higher.

Life Insurance

What is life insurance?

Life insurance provides lifelong coverage and includes a death benefit as well as a savings or investment component, known as cash value, which accumulates over time.

Who should consider life insurance?

Life insurance is suitable for individuals looking for lifelong coverage, estate planning options, and a policy that can serve as an investment or savings tool.

What are the different types of life insurance?

The main types of life insurance are whole life, universal life, and variable life. Each type offers different features, such as guaranteed cash value accumulation or investment options.

How does the cash value component work?

The cash value component of a life insurance policy grows over time, based on the premiums paid and the policy's interest rate or investment performance. Policyholders can borrow against or withdraw the cash value.

Are life insurance premiums higher than term insurance premiums?

Yes, life insurance premiums are generally higher than term insurance premiums because they provide lifelong coverage and include a savings or investment component.

Can I change my life insurance coverage amount?

Some life insurance policies, particularly universal life policies, offer flexibility to adjust the coverage amount and premiums, subject to policy terms and conditions.

General Questions

What factors should I consider when choosing between term and life insurance?

Consider your financial goals, coverage needs, budget, and the time period for which you need coverage. Term insurance is cost-effective for temporary needs, while life insurance offers lifelong protection and investment benefits.

How do I apply for a life or term insurance policy?

Contact Tejas Life Insurance to speak with an advisor. The application process typically involves completing a form, a medical exam, and underwriting to determine your premiums.

What information do I need to provide during the application?

You will need to provide personal information, including your age, health history, lifestyle habits, and financial details. This information helps determine your eligibility and premiums.

Can I have both term and life insurance policies?

Yes, you can have both term and life insurance policies. This strategy, known as laddering, allows you to tailor coverage to meet different financial needs and stages of life.

How can I determine the right coverage amount for my needs?

Consider your financial obligations, income replacement needs, debts, and future expenses like education and retirement. Consulting with a financial advisor can help you determine the appropriate coverage amount.
